Lane Selection Review Report

A human-readable review of how analyze-and-search picks a music curation lane for an uploaded photo — generated for visual inspection before deploy. This file is not imported by any app code and has no runtime effect.

1. Overview

  • Lane selection happens entirely inside one GPT-4o Vision call (analyzeImage() in gpt.ts).
  • The prompt considers: scene type, time of day, weather feeling, season, openness, motion, social context, emotional tone, color/lighting, aesthetic era, and dominant subject — plus cultural context as secondary flavor.
  • Real user location and real weather are NOT used — only what's visible in the uploaded photo.
  • Cultural/location cues are explicitly secondary flavor only — a Japanese alley is not automatically City Pop, a Korean street is not automatically K-indie, a New York street is not automatically hip-hop.
  • The backend does not silently override GPT's lane. An invalid primary_lane_id fails the request — there is no fallback default, and specifically no fallback to City Pop.

2. Lane Selection Principles

  1. Visible mood and energy beat location.
  2. Weather and openness matter — bright open-sky/water images should not auto-downshift into a nostalgic/calm lane.
  3. Indoor vs outdoor matters.
  4. Time of day matters — daytime sun, golden-hour nostalgia, neon night, rainy night, and late-night solitude are different territories.
  5. Social context matters — couple, lone person, friends, crowd all shift the lane.
  6. Motion matters — driving, walking, dancing, performing, traveling, resting point to different lanes.
  7. Aesthetic era matters — retro/analog is not the same as modern/glossy.
  8. Cultural cues are secondary, never the deciding factor alone.
  9. When two lanes conflict, a dedicated conflict-resolver block in the prompt breaks the tie.
